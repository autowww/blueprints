Roles & archetypes

Five delivery archetypes, detailed expectations, and how Scrum, Kanban, phased delivery, and XP tweak them. Full depth: roles-archetypes.md. On this page is the sticky panel at right (wide screens).

Vocabulary

  • Archetype — stable accountability pattern (what questions you answer).
  • Tweak — how a methodology changes emphasis, time-boxing, or handoffs—not a different archetype.
  • Owner / Implementer — minimal pair in SDLC.md; map to archetypes below.
  • Contributor — in sdlc/TRACKING-FOUNDATION.md, identity in the event stream (e.g. git), not a job title.

1. Demand & value

Blueprint hat: Owner (primary).

AttributeSummary
AccountabilityWhat is built next, why it matters; acceptance at boundaries.
PhasesA–B strongest; consulted in C–D; E–F for intent / go-no-go.
Anti-patternMicromanaging how the team builds—that is Build.

Detail (responsibilities)

  • Order backlog; frame outcomes; accept/reject against criteria; escalate to Steer when policy or funding blocks progress.
  • Keep priority changes visible (tracker, WBS) for traceability.

Methodology tweaks

LensTweak
ScrumNamed Product Owner; ordering + Sprint Goal + Review with stakeholders.
KanbanNo prescribed title; replenishment replaces sprint selection; demand may sit off-board.
PhasedSponsor/BA/PM front-load; baselines limit late reordering; formal UAT/sign-off.
XPCustomer onsite; frequent acceptance; often PO-shaped today.

2. Build & integrate

Blueprint hat: Implementer (primary).

AttributeSummary
AccountabilityWorking integrated product: design, code, tests, docs per team rules.
PhasesB–E core; F often with Assure.
Anti-patternSilent scope creep or skipping agreed Definition of Done.

Detail

  • Implement and integrate; maintain REQ/traceability; negotiate feasibility with Demand.
  • Own engineering quality day-to-day even when QA exists.

Methodology tweaks

LensTweak
ScrumDevelopers own Sprint Backlog, increment, Definition of Done (with team).
KanbanPull + WIP limits; role names are organizational.
PhasedRole split by phase; handoffs need strong specs and traceability.
XPPairing, TDD, CI, collective ownership, sustainable pace.

3. Flow & improvement

Blueprint hat: Often Owner/Implementer; Scrum → Scrum Master.

AttributeSummary
AccountabilityDelivery system health: impediments, ceremonies, improvement.
PhasesCross-cuts A–F via cadence.
Anti-patternProxy PO or sole technical gatekeeper.

Detail

  • Facilitate; escalate org blockers; protect focus without isolating Demand.

Methodology tweaks

LensTweak
ScrumScrum Master establishes Scrum, removes impediments, serves PO, Developers, org.
KanbanOften merged into service manager / lead; service delivery review.
PhasedPMO / phase manager; improvement driven by gates and audits.
XPOptional coach; team self-organizes around values.

4. Assure & ship

Blueprint hat: Often part of Implementer; may split to QA / release / SRE.

AttributeSummary
AccountabilityFit for release: verification, evidence, release mechanics, ops readiness.
PhasesD–F strong.
Anti-patternQuality only as a late phase if Build drops built-in quality.

Detail

  • DoD, test strategy, CI gates, release notes, rollback; compliance evidence with Steer.

Methodology tweaks

LensTweak
ScrumTeam DoD; PO accepts increment (value lens ≠ “tests green” only).
KanbanExplicit policies per column; release cadence / train.
PhasedDedicated verification phase; IV&V; formal evidence packages.
XPTDD + CI embed quality in Developers; small releases.

5. Steer & govern

Blueprint hat: Often outside Owner/Implementer; Owner may proxy sponsor.

AttributeSummary
AccountabilityFunding, portfolio, compliance, tollgates, legal constraints.
PhasesA, phase exits, F org approval.
Anti-patternDaily backlog command without escalation path—erodes Demand.

Detail

  • Charters, change control, gate sign-off; resolve escalations from Demand/Flow.

Methodology tweaks

LensTweak
ScrumOutside team; stakeholders at Review; SM escalates org impediments.
KanbanPortfolio/funding above the board; avoid starving improvement WIP.
PhasedStrong: CCB, baselines, signatures—git ≠ approval.
XPLight until regulation forces heavier Steer.

Blueprint defaults

RoleArchetypes (typical)
OwnerDemand & value; may proxy Steer; tiny team Flow.
ImplementerBuild & integrate; often Assure & ship; may share Flow.

Methodology roll-up

How each whole methodology shifts the five archetypes together (see Markdown for full narrative).

Scrum

  • Explicit PO, SM, Developers; time-boxed Sprint; Review connects Steer stakeholders.

Kanban

  • Flow and policies over named trio; portfolio Steer often separate from delivery board.

Phased delivery

  • Steer and Assure heaviest; Demand front-loaded; Build split by handoffs.

XP

  • Assure merged into Build via practices; Demand close; Steer thin unless regulated.

Agile umbrella

No sixth archetype—blend cadence (Scrum/Kanban) with XP practices; assign a person per archetype after you pick a primary rhythm.

Specialty hats

Map expansions back to archetypes: tech lead → Build; QA → Assure; DevOps/SRE → Assure + Build; security → Assure + Steer + Build; UX → Demand + Build.

Tracking

Process roles ≠ Contributor in git. POs who do not commit may be absent from commit metrics—see Agentic SDLC.
