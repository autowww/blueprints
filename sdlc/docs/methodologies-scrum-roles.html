Scrum — roles

Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Developers: accountabilities, foundation fit, event participation, and RACI-style views for planning, review, and retro. Normative terms: Scrum Guide. Canonical Markdown (edit first): roles.md · roles-archetypes.md.

Scrum Team

The Scrum Team is Product Owner + Scrum Master + Developers (everyone who creates the increment—testers, UX in the team, etc.). The Guide splits accountabilities so value ordering, process health, and build & quality are not collapsed into one overloaded job title.

Product Owner

AspectPrescriptive guidance
Accountable forMaximizing product value from the team’s work; effective Product Backlog management
Typical archetypesSponsor proxy, Orchestrator
Single accountable POOne PO per product (not a committee); may delegate edits, not accountability
Daily involvementAvailable for clarification; not required at every Daily Scrum (team choice)

Outputs: Product Goal narrative, ordered backlog, transparent items, release intent per increment.

Scrum Master

AspectPrescriptive guidance
Accountable forScrum as defined; team effectiveness
Typical archetypesOrchestrator, Quality advocate (practice & org impediments)
ServesTeam, PO, organization—coaching, teaching, change
NotProject manager assigning tasks; permanent meeting scribe only

Outputs: Impediment visibility, improvement items, facilitated events, transparency on Scrum health.

Developers

AspectPrescriptive guidance
Accountable forQuality each Sprint; Sprint Backlog plan; daily adaptation toward Sprint Goal
Typical archetypesImplementer, shared Quality advocate; rotating Daily facilitator
Cross-functionalAll skills to produce a Done increment—or honest gap and escalation

Outputs: Done increment, Sprint Backlog updates, debt visibility, estimates for planning.

Agentic SDLC

Coding agents do not replace PO acceptance, SM accountability for the process, or Developers’ ownership of quality and the Sprint Backlog plan. Trace agent work to PBIs, human-review it, and enforce DoD. See Agentic SDLC · agentic-sdlc.md.

Scaling

Multiple teams on one product add coordination (e.g. Nexus, LeSS) but keep the three accountabilities per team. Structure PO/backlog ownership so one ordered backlog per product stays unambiguous.
