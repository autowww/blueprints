Handbook

Business analysis

Understanding organizational needs, defining solutions, and managing requirements — the cross-cutting discipline that spans both PDLC and SDLC.

Source of truth: Markdown in blueprints/disciplines/product/ba/ (BABOK.md, BA-PDLC-SDLC-BRIDGE.md, knowledge-areas/).

Overview

Business analysis answers “what do stakeholders need, and does the solution satisfy those needs?” Grounded in the BABOK Guide v3 (IIBA), this blueprint covers six knowledge areas, 50+ techniques, and context-specific perspectives.

BA is descriptive, not prescriptive: use the knowledge areas and techniques that fit your team’s context.

Knowledge areas

Knowledge areaFocus
BA Planning & MonitoringPlanning the BA approach, stakeholder engagement, governance of BA work.
Strategy AnalysisUnderstanding the enterprise context, defining change strategy, assessing current/future state.
Elicitation & CollaborationGathering and confirming information from stakeholders through interviews, workshops, observation.
Requirements Life Cycle ManagementTracing, maintaining, prioritizing, and approving requirements across their lifecycle.
Requirements Analysis & Design DefinitionModeling, specifying, verifying, and validating requirements and designs.
Solution EvaluationAssessing solution performance against business needs, recommending changes.

Deep guides per knowledge area in knowledge-areas/.

PDLC & SDLC bridges

BA spans both lifecycles: Strategy Analysis maps to PDLC P1–P3, Elicitation feeds discovery, Requirements Analysis drives SDLC specification, and Solution Evaluation maps to PDLC P5–P6.

Full mapping: BA-PDLC-SDLC-BRIDGE.md.

Governance

This directory is governed by POLICY.md: do not edit casually. Project-specific BA artifacts (stakeholder registers, business cases, requirements packages) belong in the consuming repo.
