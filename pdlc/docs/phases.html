Phases P1–P6

SDLC phases A–F sit between P3 and P4 as the Build & Release engine. In mature teams, P1–P2 run in parallel with SDLC delivery (Dual-Track Agile).

P1 — Discover problem

Goal: Validate that a real, meaningful problem exists for a specific audience before investing in solutions.

Activities: User interviews, market research, competitive analysis, data mining, persona refinement, problem framing.

ArtifactAction
Problem statementClear articulation of who has the problem, what it costs them, and how you know.
Research synthesisSummary of user interviews, survey data, market signals — evidence, not opinion.
Persona validationUpdated or confirmed personas in docs/product/personas/.
Competitive landscapeHow alternatives address (or fail to address) the same problem.
Opportunity assessmentSize of the opportunity (TAM/SAM/SOM or equivalent); urgency and frequency.

Exit: Evidence (not intuition) that a meaningful, underserved problem exists. Stakeholders agree the problem is worth exploring.

P2 — Validate solution

Goal: Generate and test solution ideas to find one that addresses the validated problem — before committing engineering capacity.

Activities: Ideation, rapid prototyping, usability testing, feasibility spikes, concept testing, experiments.

ArtifactAction
Solution hypothesesExplicit: "We believe [solution] will [outcome] for [audience] because [evidence]."
Prototype(s)Low-fi or high-fi depending on risk level.
Experiment logEach experiment: hypothesis, method, results, decision (pivot/persevere).
Feasibility assessmentTech Lead's evaluation: technical risk, dependencies, complexity, architectural fit.
Usability evidenceTest results showing whether users can achieve goals with the proposed solution.

Exit: Evidence of desirability, viability, feasibility, and usability. Team ready to commit strategy and resources.

P3 — Strategize

Goal: Commit organizational resources — budget, people, timeline — to the validated solution.

Activities: Product vision, OKR setting, roadmap creation, success metrics, pricing, GTM planning, stakeholder alignment.

ArtifactAction
Product visionWhy this product exists, for whom, and what world it creates.
Success metricsMeasurable outcomes: adoption rate, retention, NPS, revenue target.
RoadmapHigh-level phased plan — what ships when, which outcomes each phase targets.
Go-to-market planLaunch strategy, channels, positioning, pricing.
Stage-gate reviewFormal go/kill/pivot decision with criteria.

Exit: Initiative is funded, staffed, and prioritized. Success metrics defined. Handoff to SDLC Phase A.

SDLC Phases A–F — Build & Release

The existing blueprints/sdlc/ phases handle delivery: Discover → Specify → Design → Build → Verify → Release. The product trio stays engaged via C1 Align and C4 Inspect ceremonies.

P4 — Launch

Goal: Make the product available to the target market and activate go-to-market.

Activities: Beta management, GA rollout, marketing activation, sales enablement, support training, docs publishing.

ArtifactAction
Launch checklistCompleteness: docs, support, marketing, legal, monitoring.
Rollout planPhased rollout: internal → beta → GA. Feature flags, rollback criteria.
Support playbookKnown issues, FAQ, escalation paths.
Monitoring baselineDashboards for P5 metrics established and baselined.

Exit: Product available to intended audience. Monitoring active. Support ready.

P5 — Grow

Goal: Drive adoption, measure outcomes, and iterate based on real-world usage data.

Activities: Analytics review, A/B testing, funnel optimization, retention analysis, feature iteration, NPS/CSAT surveys.

ArtifactAction
Metrics dashboardLive tracking of P3 success metrics: adoption, retention, engagement, revenue, NPS.
Experiment logOngoing A/B tests and iterations: hypothesis → result → decision.
Iteration backlogImprovements flowing from data back into SDLC Phase A (continuous loop).
Growth reportPeriodic summary: what's working, what's not, strategic recommendations.

Exit: Product health targets met, or strategic decision: pivot (back to P1), invest more (P5), or harvest (P6).

P6 — Mature / Sunset

Goal: Manage the product through maturity and eventual retirement.

Activities: Lifecycle assessment, maintenance optimization, harvest/invest/divest decisions, sunset planning, migration, EOL communications.

ArtifactAction
Lifecycle assessmentCurrent stage, investment recommendation, competitive position.
Sunset planTimeline, migration path, customer communications, data retention.
Migration guideFor customers moving to a replacement or alternative.
Post-mortemWhat worked, what didn't, lessons for future products.

Exit: Product retired (removed, support ended, data handled) or repositioned (back to P1 with new framing).
